**Facebook**

> Boosting optimises for the metrics that feel good. Advertising optimises for
> the ones that pay you.
>
> They're not the same tool at different difficulty levels. When you boost,
> Meta's default objective is engagement - it goes and finds the people most
> likely to react, comment or share. Those are real people. They're just very
> rarely buyers. People who like posts and people who buy condos are different
> audiences.
>
> Three questions worth answering honestly:
>
> 1. Do you know what a customer inquiry currently costs you?
> 2. Can you name your best-performing creative of the last three months, and
>    why it won?
> 3. If you doubled the budget tomorrow, do you know what would happen?
>
> If the answer to all three is no, you've been boosting - not advertising.
> That's fixable, and the fix usually starts by leaving the budget exactly
> where it is.

## Posting notes

**Order.** Lead with 01, then alternate proof and value: 02, 07, 05, 03, 08,
06, 04. Don't post the three case studies back to back - it reads as bragging.
Proof lands harder when something useful sits between each one.

**Cadence.** Two or three a week is plenty. Eight posts is roughly a month.

**Hashtags.** Instagram only - Facebook hashtags do close to nothing. Keep to
6-8 and rotate; identical tag blocks on every post get suppressed.

**Comments.** Reply to every one within the hour if you can. The engagement
matters, but more importantly it's the same behaviour we sell: a fast reply is
what turns interest into an inquiry.

**Sizes.** 1080x1080 works for the IG feed and FB. For Stories, place the
square on a plain `#1B1B1B` or `#FAF8F5` 1080x1920 background rather than
stretching it.
